Mycelium

whisper a sentence — a creature grows from how it feels

An anonymous whisper is read by a language model and grown into a one-of-a-kind pixel spore, then let loose in a shared colony where everyone's feelings meet and slowly rub off on each other. The same sentence always grows the same spore.

Problem
Young adults in transition are easy to reach online but often feel disconnected. Platforms surface content, not how a group actually feels.
Approach
Turn anonymous disclosure into a living ecology: input → AI reading → morphology → social simulation → shared field.
Contribution
A way to feel present with others without talking — connection shown as colour exchange, care built in as an ecological rule, identity fixed as deterministic form.
Role & status
Concept, design & full-stack build · deployed web prototype · formative evaluation in progress.
✳

Why “Mycelium”

The whole thing borrows from mycorrhizal networks — the underground web of fungal threads (the mycelium) that links the trees of a forest, sometimes called the “wood-wide web.” Through it, trees quietly trade sugar and nutrients, pass chemical signals, and older mother trees keep seedlings alive. It's connection that is slow, shared, and out of sight — the opposite of a feed. Below is the real network it's named after — every claim cited:

02

What it is

It isn't a mood diary, an AI companion, or a feed. It's a small shared world where private feelings stick around as living creatures.

Everything here is procedural — no drawn sprites, no image model. The reading of a sentence seeds a deterministic random stream, and that stream decides the creature's outline, palette, sharpness and behaviour. So an anonymous feeling turns into something you can name, claim, share and come back to, without ever attaching who you are.

04

Emotion becomes form

Every whisper is read into one of six emotion families, each with its own base hue. Then the reading bends the form: intensity widens the palette from near-monochrome to rainbow, density sets how solid it is, agitation sets the jitter, and a second emotion blends in one accent. Same sentence, same creature — but no two feelings land the same.

05

How the colony behaves

Connection as colour

逐格染色 — a tendril in colour, not a line

When two compatible creatures meet, they dye each other starting from where they touch — strongest at the contact seam — and pass small matter tiles back and forth while bonded, breathing in sync.

Mother trees

母树 — care as an ecological rule

A creature that has been around long enough starts supporting an isolated one: it grows visibly larger, sways, flickers, and feeds it a slow one-way stream of matter. Nothing you press sets this off.

Marks that stay

永久杂交 — changed, not fused

A long enough encounter leaves a small, permanent trace of the other's palette. The creature keeps its own identity; the relationship just stays written on it.

07

From screen to substrate

The whisper doesn't have to stop at the screen. In the gallery installation, the colony's pooled emotion drives a living fungus, so the same anonymous sentence sets the growing conditions for a real culture. The software bridge is built and live; the physical cultivation is specced and staged.
